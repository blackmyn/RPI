Acting style and Reception

Nouveau Shamanic

Nouveau Shamanic is a style of acting developed by Cage, aimed at unleashing one's imagination without inhibition to avoid the feeling of being ingenuous. Dating back to pre-Christian times, tribal shamans would enact the problems faced by their communities to seek solutions. They achieved this by entering a trance-like state, utilizing their imagination to manifest the challenges. This forms the core of Nouveau Shamanic.

Examples

Nicolas Cage describes the process as an exploration of how to enrich one's imagination in a healthy manner, allowing actors to embody characters fully, transcending the mere act of performance. Examples of this method can be seen in various Cage films. In "Vampire's Kiss," Cage transitions from method acting to Nouveau Shamanic, as evidenced by his impulsive recitation of the alphabet to his psychiatrist. "Raising Arizona" demonstrates the physical intensity inherent in Nouveau Shamanic, where Cage's unrestrained portrayal adds a unique dimension to the audience's experience. "Ghost Rider: Spirit of Vengeance" showcases how this method translates onto the screen, with Cage's spontaneous decisions fueled by a natural energy.
